  title: Privacy, Security, and Trust in Blockchain-Secured Edge/Fog Services
  authors: S. Ida Evangeline
  abstract: As edge and fog computing emerge as foundational architectures for latency-sensitive and data-intensive applications—ranging
    from smart cities and autonomous systems to healthcare and industrial IoT—ensuring privacy, security, and trust across
    distributed environments has become a pressing concern. In this chapter, the focus is on the ways blockchain technology,
    merged with semantic annotation frameworks, can strengthen reliability, integrity, and autonomy of edge/fog services.
    It proposes a multidimensional study of privacy-protecting mechanisms, decentralized security infrastructures and dynamic
    trust models that suit resource limited, heterogeneous computing environments. The chapter explores lightweight consensus
    protocols, smart contracts, decentralized identity systems that can help the secure communication and access control at
    edge. It also discusses how services can facilitate discoverability, context-aware policy enforcement and trust propagation
    using semantic ontologies/metadata that is machine interpretable.
