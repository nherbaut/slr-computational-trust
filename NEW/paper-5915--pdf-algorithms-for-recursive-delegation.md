  title: '[PDF] Algorithms for recursive delegation'
  authors: Juan Afanador, Murilo S. Baptista, Nir Oren
  abstract: Task delegation is essential to many applications, ranging from outsourcing of work to the design of routing protocols.
    Much research in computational trust has been devoted to the generation of policies to determine which agent should the
    task be delegated to, given the agent’s past behaviour. Suc h work, however, does not consider the possibility of the
    agent delegating the task onwards, inducing a chain of delegation events before the task is finally executed. In this
    paper, we consider the process of delegation chain formation, introducing a new algorithm based on quitting games to cater
    for recursive scenarios. We evaluate our proposal under various network topologies, consistently demonstrating its superiority
    with respect to recursive adaptations of existing multi-armed bandit algorithms.
