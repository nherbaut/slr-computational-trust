  title: Insider Threats and Social Engineering in Healthcare
  authors: Olatunde Ayomide Olasehan
  abstract: The healthcare sector is increasingly vulnerable to cybersecurity threats, particularly from insider threats and
    social engineering attacks that target sensitive patient data. Insider threats, originating from individuals with authorized
    access, can result in data theft and unauthorized access to electronic health records (EHRs). Social engineering tactics
    exploit trust to manipulate personnel into disclosing protected health information. While cloud trust models enhance security
    in interconnected environments, challenges remain in balancing security with privacy rights and regulatory compliance.
    Future directions include the use of artificial intelligence for predictive threat analysis, with careful consideration
    of ethical implications. The evolving nature of attack tactics necessitates adaptive defense mechanisms. Collaboration
    among stakeholders is crucial to establish frameworks and promote resilience. A comprehensive approach that integrates
    technology, training, and policy is essential to safeguard patient data and ensure operational continuity.
