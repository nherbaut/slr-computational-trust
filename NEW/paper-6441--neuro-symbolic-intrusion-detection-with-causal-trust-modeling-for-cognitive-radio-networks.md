  title: Neuro-Symbolic Intrusion Detection with Causal Trust Modeling for Cognitive Radio Networks
  authors: T.Jayaprakash Reddy, P. Shyamala Bharathi
  abstract: The proposed Neuro-Symbolic Causal Trust Intrusion Detection (NSCT-ID) model is an improvement of the security
    of Cognitive Radio Networks (CRNs) since it incorporates neural learning, symbolic reasoning, and causal inference. The
    system encodes spectral and temporal features with a neural encoder, verifies anomalies with symbolic rules and measures
    inter-node dependencies with causal trust modeling. Benchmark Experimental testing on benchmark data sets shows better
    performance with an average accuracy of 97.3%, precision of 96.9% and recall of 97.6% which is better than classical machine
    learning, deep learning, and graph-based models. The hybrid architecture is effective in detecting the direct attacks
    as well as coordinated attacks and is interpretable. Real-time applicability is assured by low detection latency ($(\approx
    12-14 \mathrm{~ms})$ and high throughput ($\gt 95 \%$). The findings confirm the hypothesis that the effectiveness of
    statistical intelligence and logical reasoning in combination can greatly enhance the resistance to detection, minimizing
    the false positives, and making decisions of trustworthiness in the dynamic and adversarial CRN setting.
