  title: Enhancing Human-Robot Collaboration through Neuro-Symbolic and Neuromorphic AI
  authors: J. Balamurugan, Kumar A. Senthil, E.U. Iniyan, T Dharanika, M. Gangadhar Rao, R. Senthamil Selvan
  abstract: Human-Robot Interaction (HRI) requires real-time flexibility, emotional intelligence, and concise communication
    to facilitate efficient cooperation. Conventional AI methodologies, although potent, often exhibit inflexible behaviours,
    sluggish learning processes, and diminished reliability in dynamic contexts. This study investigates brain-inspired artificial
    intelligence architectures, including spiking neural networks (SNNs), predictive coding models, neuromorphic computing
    & cognitive frameworks such as ACT-R and SOAR, to overcome these constraints. A hybrid neuro-symbolic framework is presented
    to improve decision-making, learning efficiency, & explainability in human-robot interaction. Comprehensive simulations
    using datasets such as the Cornell Activity Dataset (for gesture recognition) and BabyLM (for language grounding) inside
    Gazebo and MuJoCo environments substantiate the methodology. Findings indicate that spiking neural networks using neuromorphic
    hardware attain a 35% decrease in latency and a sevenfold reduction in energy consumption relative to conventional deep
    learning methods. Neuro-symbolic models get 92% accuracy in few-shot learning and enhance human interpretability scores
    by 60%. Research on trust calibration indicates that explainable models significantly improve user trust in collaborative
    and emergency response activities. This research establishes a framework for creating brain-inspired, energy-efficient,
    and reliable robotic systems for practical human-centered applications.
