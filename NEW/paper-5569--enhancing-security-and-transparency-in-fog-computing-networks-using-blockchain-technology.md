  title: Enhancing Security and Transparency in Fog Computing Networks using Blockchain Technology
  authors: M Rudra Kumar, N Karthik Narayan, Deepika
  abstract: As smart transportation infrastructure and vehicle-to-everything (V2X) communications become increasingly widespread,
    maintaining secure, trusted interactions between vehicles, roadside units (RSUs), and infrastructure has become a major
    challenge. In today's vehicle networks, the ongoing exchange of data is critical to network stability and accuracy. If
    these data are compromised, they can adversely affect road safety and system performance. We address this challenge by
    working on the integration of trust management systems with blockchain technology and fog computing to enhance the reliability,
    transparency, and security of vehicular communication. This work reviews current trust evaluation models, investigating
    how RSUs make trust decisions and update their trust scores based on human behavior patterns and communication accuracy.
    Particular emphasis is placed on the role of decentralized fog nodes in real-time validation of data and decision making
    at the network edge to reduce latency and reduce the dependence on central servers. Also a blockchain ledger can be used
    to store the trust updates and behavior logs in a tamper-proof and auditable way to strengthen transparency and resistance
    of malicious actions. Synthetically generated datasets, derived from simulation tools such as SUMO, are used to study
    the behavior of the traffic systems under various traffic scenarios. Also a study is undertaken on the application of
    machine learning algorithms for anomaly detection and trust prediction. Finally, some important limitations concerning
    scalability, privacy and computational burden are addressed, which would yield promising prospects for further research
    in lightweight consensus mechanisms and adaptive trust models. Also this survey also serves as a full-fledged assessment
    of an advanced framework to implement secure and trust-based traffic systems that would be feasible in any smart city
    context.
