  title: Secure and Scalable AI Pipelines for Network Automation in Highly Regulated SaaS Environments
  authors: Harika Rama Tulasi Karatapu, Vishal Gudhka
  abstract: In highly controlled SaaS environments, network automation with AI has a tremendous positive impact on efficiency
    and stability. However, AI model attacks in SaaS CI/CD pipelines pose a significant security threat. This paper solves
    these problems by exploring evasion attacks on ML-based Network Intrusion Detection Systems (NIDS) and data poisoning
    at model training. We discuss defensive strategies that involve Zero Trust models, such as AI-based policy enforcement
    and micro-segmentation, and emphasize mitigation techniques like adversarial training, data sanitization, ensemble learning,
    and continuous retraining of the model in CI/CD pipelines. Our discussion also emphasizes AI-powered cyber resilience
    capabilities, such as automated threat identification, rapid response, and self-healing, that minimize downtime and ensure
    compliance in multi-tenant cloud environments. The resulting framework provides actionable guidelines for developing stable,
    AI-enabled network automation solutions that can withstand sophisticated adversarial attacks in today's SaaS implementations.
    Overall, this research offers a robust foundation for defending AI-enabled automation against cutting-edge threats and
    maintaining stringent regulatory compliance.
