  title: 'Cybersecurity Challenges and Strategies in Critical Infrastructure Systems: A Comprehensive Survey'
  authors: Mihai-Alexandru Jibotean, Ovidiu Stan
  abstract: This paper offers a comprehensive survey of cybersecurity challenges and countermeasures in critical infrastructure
    systems (CIs), focusing on Industrial Control Systems (ICS) and Operational Technology (OT) networks. We analyze significant
    cyber incidents, such as the Ukraine power grid incidents, and malware like Stuxnet, to understand the evolving threat
    landscape. The paper categorize cyber threats, identifies key actors, and presents current and emerging technologies such
    as Zero Trust Architecture, Artificial Intelligence, and Blockchain. Eacx technologiy is discussed in the context of applicability,
    advantages, and limitation in CI environments. Additionally, we propose a layer evaluation framework for integrating the
    Zero Trust model into ICS environments and address the difficulties involved in applying AI for OT network monitoring.
    Implementation barriers – including legacy system contraints, lack of interoperability, and inconsistent international
    standards – are critically assessed. Finally, this paper highlights important directions for future research to enhance
    the protection of critical infrastructure against evolving and ongoing cyber threats.
