  title: Designing Conversational Agents in Copilot Studio for Enterprise Automation and Compliance
  authors: Sarat Piridi, Nataraja kumar Koduri, Sharan Asundi
  abstract: The presented paper examines how conversational agents can be implemented and designed in the Microsoft Copilot
    Studio with a special consideration given to how they may be used to automate enterprise operations as well as comply
    with regulatory issues. These agents enhance the productivity, teamwork and confidence in human-AI settings in the integration
    of intelligent task decomposition, contextual awareness and user-role adaptivity. Research data demonstrates that Copilot
    Studio Agents are more accurate, efficient and user friendly, as compared to static flows. Other issues pinpointed in
    the paper include overdependence, ethical issues and openness. Our simulations, tests of performance evaluation, and methods
    of trust calibration allow us to build a complete picture of creating solid and enterprise-level conversational agents
    according to the perspective of compliance and automation.
