  title: Development of Structure For Reliability Kind of Assessment Through Artificial Intelligence
  authors: Padmaja Kadiri, A. Brahmareddy, Satish Narayanasamy, Alankrita Aggarwal, Ahmed Jalal Fakher, Aqeel Al-Hilali
  abstract: 'The transformative potential of AI raises serious moral questions for companies and calls into question the need
    for trust. To address this, several ethical frameworks are commonly proposed, if only in theory. AI is also necessary
    for workers to appropriately measure trust. There aren’t many studies that address how an ethical framework may help employees
    calibrate their practical trust. To ensure trust calibration and reduce this gap, research is being done on an ethical
    framework that centres on AI safety and reliability. Finally, an assessment of the efficacy of the deployed framework
    is conducted through interviews conducted at a multinational automotive supplier. This ethical foundation thus led to
    a significant rise in trust. This is a groundbreaking finding: workers are willing to tolerate a lesser level of AI safety
    as long as AI reliability is preserved.'
