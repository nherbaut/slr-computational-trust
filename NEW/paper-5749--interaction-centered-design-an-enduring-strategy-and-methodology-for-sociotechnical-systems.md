  title: 'Interaction‐Centered Design: An Enduring Strategy and Methodology for Sociotechnical Systems'
  authors: Ming Hou, Scott Fang, Wenbi Wang, Philip S. E. Farrell
  abstract: With collective human–machine intelligence, the human–machine symbiosis (HMS) technologies are prevalent in society
    today and capable of solving complex problems. However, the trend raises important questions about the benefits, complications,
    liabilities, risks, and trust associated with increasing intelligence and adaptivity in these sociotechnical systems given
    both humans and machines have limitations. It is even more challenging when we are facing insufficient data, incomplete
    information, indeterministic conditions, and inexhaustive solutions for uncertain actions. To provide guidance to understand
    and mitigate the potential risks associated with employing these HMS technologies, the evolution of design strategy and
    methodology for intelligent adaptive systems (IASs) was reviewed first. An interaction-centered design (ICD) framework,
    an associated set of methodologies and roadmap, and a related trust model called IMPACTS were then introduced as an enduring
    strategy and appropriate solution. Validation studies on the utility and effectiveness of the ICD approach were performed
    through real-world technology demonstration and evaluation activities. Challenges in integrating the ICD approach into
    systems engineering and validation processes are discussed along with the future directions for research, development,
    and exploitation of IAS technologies.
