  title: Deep Learning-Enabled Monitoring of Dialogue Quality in Conversational AI Systems
  authors: E. Lakshaya, Purva Borse, Ashokkumar C
  abstract: Dialogue breakdown detection, defined as the identification of miscommunication points where AI assistant responses
    fail to fulfill user intent, constitutes a critical challenge for robust conversational AI systems. This study presents
    a dialogue-level framework for detecting and categorizing breakdowns in human–AI interactions, utilizing a unified corpus
    derived from the BETOLD and DBDC5 datasets. The dataset comprises dialogue instances annotated with both binary breakdown
    labels and three-class error-type categories, with each instance incorporating a focal system utterance identified as
    a potential breakdown point. Model inputs are generated by concatenating preceding dialogue context with the focal utterance
    to capture long-range dependencies and context-specific patterns. A DeBERTa-v3-base transformer encoder is fine-tuned
    in a multi-task configuration to simultaneously predict dialogue-level breakdowns and associated error types. Comparative
    evaluation includes BERT-base and BiLSTM with attention architectures. The DeBERTa-v3-base model achieves superior binary
    breakdown detection with an accuracy of 0.912, F1-score of 0.898, and ROC-AUC of 0.940, surpassing BERT-base (F1 0.761)
    and BiLSTM + Attention (F1 0.716). For error-type classification, the model attains a Macro F1 of 0.871 and Weighted F1
    of 0.902, demonstrating robust performance across entity-level and dialogue-flow error categories. Additional evaluation
    of threshold/probability metrics, training convergence, reproducibility, and inference efficiency confirms stable performance
    and low-latency operation. The results demonstrate that context-aware transformer architectures significantly improve
    the detection and classification of dialogue breakdowns. This framework provides a reliable foundation for real-time monitoring
    and intervention in AI dialogue systems, supporting accurate, low-latency corrective mechanisms and enhancing overall
    human–AI interaction quality.
