  title: '[PDF] Fostering critical AI competency: a structural equation model of pre-service teachers’ trust and actual AI
    use in higher education'
  authors: Chaiyos Paiwithayasiritham, Kemmanat Mingsiritham, Waraporn Sinthaworn, Chularat Busabong
  abstract: In recent years, education has experienced rapid change due to the rise of artificial intelligence (AI). This
    study analyzed how twelve interconnected factors, based on technology acceptance and trust theories, influence trust in
    AI for learning (TL) and actual AI use (AU) among pre-service teachers in Thailand. Using a quantitative design, with
    data collected from 260 pre-service teachers through purposive sampling based on prior AI experience. A 60-item, 5-point
    Likert-scale questionnaire, validated through pilot testing and internal consistency analysis (α=0.82–0.91). Data was
    analyzed using structural equation modeling (SEM) with maximum likelihood estimation.<strong> </strong>The model showed
    very good fit (χ²/df=1.601, root mean square error of approximation (RMSEA)=0.049) and explained 90.80% of behavioral
    intention (BI) and 71.20% of AU. Results indicated that cognitive load regulation (CLR) was the strongest predictor of
    TL (β=0.786, p&lt;0.001), while responsible AI awareness (RAA) also showed positive effect. In contrast, AI self-efficacy
    (ASE) in a negative way (β=-0.159, p=0.009). The primary predictor of AU was BI (β=0.884, p&lt;0.001). These findings
    highlight the importance of AI education systems, which will reduce teachers’ cognitive load and contribute to an improved
    ethical AI literacy in teacher training institutions.
