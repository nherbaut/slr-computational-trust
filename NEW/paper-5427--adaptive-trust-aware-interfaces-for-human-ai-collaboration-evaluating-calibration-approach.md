  title: 'Adaptive Trust Aware Interfaces for Human AI Collaboration: Evaluating Calibration Approach'
  authors: Snehal Rathi, Saanchi Itkelwar, Naman Chordia
  abstract: Trust miscalibration when users either undertrust accurate recommendations or over-trust flawed outputssubstantially
    decreases the effectiveness of human-AI collaboration. While there exist various ways to measure trust, it is rare to
    compare adaptive interface strategies in a systematic manner. We investigate five different trust calibration approaches
    in a synthetic benchmark (Trust-Bench) that spans three trust states across three domains. These include a noadaptation
    baseline, rule-based heuristics, two variants of reinforcement learning (RL) trained with Proximal Policy Optimization
    (PPO), and a hybrid approach. Rule-based approaches performed the best among the five tested, and all adaptive strategies
    outperformed the baseline. Despite extensive training, the RL approaches did not learn effective conditional adaptations-using
    persuasive interfaces for under-trust users inconsistently, and failing to activate cautious interfaces in overtrust scenarios
    despite explicit incentives for doing so. Detailed analysis identifies weak reward signal strength for adaptation and
    difficulty in learning multi-condition logic as primary failure modes. Results show that, while deterministic approaches
    provide reliable trust calibration, algorithmic limitations are essential in guiding further development.
