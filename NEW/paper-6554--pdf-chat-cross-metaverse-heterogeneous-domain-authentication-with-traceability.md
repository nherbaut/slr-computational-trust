  title: '[PDF] CHAT: Cross-Metaverse Heterogeneous Domain Authentication with Traceability'
  authors: Chenxi Wu, Hongfeng Gao, Youliang Tian, Kun Niu, Axin Xiang
  abstract: As the metaverse evolves toward a large-scale multi-domain ecosystem, in which different domains may rely on different
    trust models, avatar cross-metaverse access increasingly depends on prior authentication between heterogeneous domains.
    Existing schemes are typically developed under homogeneous assumptions and cannot directly support domain-level privacy-preserving
    authentication. To address this gap, we propose CHAT, a cross-metaverse heterogeneous domain authentication protocol with
    traceability. CHAT enables a domain to prove its legitimacy to another heterogeneous domain while delivering endorsement
    information for an internal member requesting cross-domain access. CHAT hides the concrete identity of a domain during
    authentication, while still allowing the FA to recover the real domain when malicious behavior arises. Security analysis
    shows that CHAT achieves authentication, anonymity, and traceability under the assumed security of the underlying cryptographic
    primitives, and resists replay attacks, impersonation attacks, credential tampering, malicious endorsement abuse, and
    key-escrow-related risks. Experimental results show that the heterogeneous-domain authentication phase takes 28.55 ms
    on average and introduces 1189 bytes of communication overhead, indicating that CHAT provides enhanced security properties
    with acceptable overhead for practical domain-level deployment.
