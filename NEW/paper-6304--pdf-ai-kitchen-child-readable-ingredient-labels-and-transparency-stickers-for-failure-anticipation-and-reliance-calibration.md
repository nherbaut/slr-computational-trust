  title: '[PDF] AI Kitchen: Child-Readable Ingredient Labels and Transparency Stickers for Failure Anticipation and Reliance
    Calibration'
  authors: Hanyu Wang
  abstract: Children increasingly meet AI inside creative tools, yet may treat model behavior as magic and read confidence
    as correctness. We built AI Kitchen, a web-based make–test–share experience that turns a tiny training set into ingredients
    and makes coverage gaps visible at decision time. An Ingredient Label summarizes the selected recipe, shows coverage bars
    for conditions (e.g., low light, motion blur, look-alikes), and generates deterministic, child-readable predicted-failure
    hints from missing coverage. During testing, a predict-before-reveal step asks children to guess whether the model will
    be right, wrong, or uncertain; a Confidence Trap then presents controlled high-confidence-wrong and low-confidence-right
    cases and asks children to trust, verify, or retrain. When sharing an artifact, the system exports a compact Transparency
    Sticker that travels with the artifact and highlights blind spots plus the reminder High confidence ≠ correct. We report
    the implemented prototype and instrumentation, and outline a PLANNED formative study (target N=10, ages 8–12) using log-based
    measures of failure anticipation, transparency use, and reliance decisions.
