  title: AI-Enabled Trustworthiness in Edge Zero Trust Architectures
  authors: Vibha Negi, Pramod Negi
  abstract: Edge computing platforms are extensively incorporating the power of AI (Artificial Intelligence). AI is being
    leveraged to support applications that have latency, autonomy and reliability requirements. But the distributed and heterogeneous
    nature of these edge computing platforms makes it difficult to rely on traditional security assumptions based on the cloud.
    Zero Trust Architecture (ZTA) has been recognized as the dominant security architecture, with a focus on verification
    and least privilege. However, its implementation in the edge environment is in its infancy [3], [4]. This paper assesses
    how AI currently contributes to Zero Trust enforcement in edge environments along with identifying key gaps that persist
    irrespective of these advances. By analyzing current AI-enabled mechanisms, broader trustworthiness dimensions and their
    real-world limitations, the paper highlights challenges related to fragmented trust signals, episodic verification, incomplete
    lifecycle awareness and the implicit trust placed in AI components themselves. The analysis points to the need to view
    AI-enabled Zero Trust at the edge as an integrated trust problem rather than a collection of isolated techniques, providing
    a foundation aimed at more robust, context-aware trust models in future edge systems [1], [2].
