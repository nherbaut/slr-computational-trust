  title: 'Explainable Intelligence for Ethical Human-Centric AI: Trust Modeling Using Gradient Boosting'
  authors: Shikha Khullar, Chennaiah Kate, A. Srinivasa Rao, Umme Hani Sharrif, Anjula Gupta, S. Kaliappan
  abstract: The accelerated adoption of artificial intelligence in human decision-making processes has highlighted the importance
    of human-centric and ethical artificial intelligence systems that are transparent, trustworthy, and accountable. A key
    part of adoption of such systems is trust, which determines how humans value, trust and interact with AI assisted decisions.
    In this paper, we propose an explainable and ethical machine learning framework to address this challenge, i.e., trust
    modeling. The research uses the Human-AI Interaction dataset that describes behavioural, cognitive, and ethical characteristics
    of human trust in AI systems. An Explainable Gradient Boosting model for classifying trust in low, medium, and high is
    developed which allows intuitive and interpretable trust assessment. The classification accuracy of the proposed approach
    is 92 % overall, which shows the power in classification based on the complex data of mixed types of interactions. To
    ensure transparency and ethical accountability, explainability mechanisms of SHAP-based feature attribution are incorporated
    to provide both global- and instancelevel feature attribution information for trust prediction. The results underline
    the effectiveness of a combination of highperformance ensemble learning with explainable intelligence, which provides
    a reliable and human-centric solution to the deployment of ethical AI in trust-sensitive applications.
