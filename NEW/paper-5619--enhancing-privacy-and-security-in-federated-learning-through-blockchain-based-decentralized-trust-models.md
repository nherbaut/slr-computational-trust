  title: Enhancing Privacy and Security in Federated Learning Through Blockchain-based Decentralized Trust Models
  authors: Peiqi Li, Jianguo Chen, Yuting Fan, BoDong Wang, Weijian You, Changlin Yang
  abstract: Federated learning (FL) is a distributed machine learning paradigm that enables collaborative model training across
    decentralized datasets, preserving data privacy. However, the lack of mechanisms to ensure trust and security in federated
    learning systems remains a significant challenge. This paper proposes a blockchain-based decentralized framework designed
    to address these issues by incorporating blockchain technology to establish a secure, transparent, and trustworthy federated
    learning environment. The framework leverages a consortium blockchain to facilitate model aggregation, reputation assessment,
    and reward distribution through smart contracts and consensus protocols. Data owners, as federated learning participants,
    retain control over their local datasets, training local models and sharing gradients for global model updates. The proposed
    system ensures transparency and integrity by recording training results, reputation scores, and rewards on an immutable
    blockchain ledger. Furthermore, the paper details the design of several smart contracts to manage federated learning tasks,
    participant selection, and incentive mechanisms, thereby promoting fairness and motivating high-quality contributions.
    A parameter-aggregated blockchain structure, incorporating Merkle trees and proof-of-work (PoW), ensures the consistency
    and security of the shared model data. The results demonstrate that the proposed framework provides a robust solution
    for decentralized, trustworthy federated learning, offering significant potential for privacy-preserving applications
    in various fields.
