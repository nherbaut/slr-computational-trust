  title: A Privacy-Preserving Federated Learning and Trust Scoring Framework for Secure Digital Service Marketplaces
  authors: N Prajwalasimha S, Tarun, Vinayaka V M, Vivek Shivappa Gunari, Swayam Sidnale
  abstract: Digital service marketplaces rely heavily on behavioral analytics to evaluate trustworthiness between customers
    and service providers. However, existing centralized reputation systems expose sensitive user data and remain vulnerable
    to review manipulation, Sybil attacks, and unfair rating behaviors. This work proposes a privacy-preserving and trust-aware
    marketplace framework that integrates federated learning, multi-factor trust scoring, and anomaly-based fraud detection.
    Federated learning enables decentralized model training on user devices without sharing raw interaction logs, while a
    weighted trust model computes transparent and interpretable reliability scores. Additionally, an Isolation Forest-based
    anomaly detector identifies malicious behaviors such as rating abuse and price manipulation in real time. Experimental
    results demonstrate competitive model performance with an accuracy of 84.7%, approximately 85% reduction in centralized
    data exposure, and a fraud detection true positive rate of 85%. The system confirms that secure decentralized intelligence
    and adversarial resiliency can be jointly achieved for reliable digital service ecosystems.
