  title: Deep Neural Network and Zero Trust Principles for Intelligent Drone Management
  authors: Saad Qasim Abbas, Faris Hassan Taha Hussain, Ahmed Rasol Hasson, Waleed Hameed, Israa Abed Jawad, Ahmed Sudani
  abstract: As more people become interested in machine learning applications and solutions, crucial decisions involving systems
    engineering must start considering risks that have not previously been identified. These risks come from potential risks
    associated with collecting machine learning (ML) data, training, and ML-based conclusions. Even cyber-physical systems
    like drones and automated factories, automated vehicles, and financial investment systems have benefited from this form
    of risk analysis. This type of risk analysis has benefited the development of virtual assistants. Consequently, the work
    aims to investigate and address the potential risks of increasingly automated systems by using sophisticated ML techniques
    in systems engineering. The research, therefore, provides a zero-trust model with a deep neural network (also known as
    ZTM-DNN) in a smart agricultural system. In this technique, zero-trust concepts are applied to the management of drones
    in real-time to achieve smart farming. The suggested model is tested via simulation to determine whether it should be
    recommended in the real-time, physical agricultural system. The simulation results show that the suggested model compared
    to the existing methods, increased efficiency in security management by $\mathbf{9 8. 5 6 \%}$.
