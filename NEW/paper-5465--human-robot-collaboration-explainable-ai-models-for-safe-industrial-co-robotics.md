  title: 'Human–Robot Collaboration: Explainable AI Models for Safe Industrial Co-Robotics'
  authors: Anurag Shrivastava, Layth Hussein, Navdeep Singh, Kanchan Yaday, Gudapalli Karuna, Shashi Prakash Dwivedi
  abstract: This paper addresses the intersection of human-robot collaboration (HRC) and Explainable Artificial Intelligence
    (XAI) with the explicit aim of improving safety, transparency, and operational effectiveness in industrial co-robotics.
    We argue that achieving safe, efficient human-robot teams in shared industrial workspaces requires XAI systems that (a)
    render robot intent and internal state comprehensible to heterogeneous human operators, (b) support real-time, actionable
    explanations for decision-making and failure recovery, and (c) preserve formal safety guarantees while maintaining acceptable
    task performance. The manuscript synthesizes recent empirical results and theoretical frameworks from HRC, XAI, reinforcement
    learning, and human factors, identifies recurring failure modes and their explanatory needs, and proposes a layered architectural
    blueprint that combines (i) interpretable perception and intent-prediction modules, (ii) hybrid symbolic-statistical planning
    with counterfactual and causal explanation generators, and (iii) human-centered explanation interfaces adapted to operator
    expertise and workload. We further present an evaluation protocol that integrates objective safety metrics (e.g., time-to-stop,
    proximity violation rates), explanation quality measures (fidelity, usefulness, causability), and human factors outcomes
    (situation awareness, trust calibration, recovery accuracy). The paper concludes with concrete research directions to
    reconcile the trade-offs between transparency and real-time safety, including standards for explanation latency, benchmarks
    for explanation-driven fault recovery, and guidelines for regulatory compliance in industrial settings.
