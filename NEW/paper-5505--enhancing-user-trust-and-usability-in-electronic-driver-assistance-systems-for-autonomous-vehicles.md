  title: Enhancing User Trust and Usability in Electronic Driver Assistance Systems for Autonomous Vehicles
  authors: Patrick A. Onwuka, Sergey S. Shadrin
  abstract: This paper investigates the design and evaluation of human-machine interface (HMI) principles within advanced
    driver assistance systems (ADAS) and autonomous vehicles, focusing on enhancing user trust, safety, and usability. With
    the rapid adoption of self-driving and electric vehicles, effective interaction between human drivers and automation is
    critical to system acceptance and road safety. We propose an adaptive multimodal interface that dynamically responds to
    driver attention and cognitive load, integrating visual, auditory, and haptic feedback to optimize situational awareness.
    The proposed system architecture incorporates real-time physiological monitoring, machine learning-based state classification,
    and context-aware feedback adaptation mechanisms. Through comprehensive analysis of existing trust models and interface
    design principles, we develop a framework that addresses current limitations in ADAS human-machine interface. The architecture
    includes modules for driver state assessment, dynamic feedback generation, and trust calibration, with emphasis on transparency
    and predictable system behavior.
