  title: Human-AI Collaboration with Decision Control and Adaptive Trust for Enhancing User Perceptions
  authors: A Sasikumar, Logesh Ravi, Malathi Devarajan, A Selvalakshmi, Harishankar K Nair, Ali Wagdy Mohamed, V Subramaniyaswamy
  abstract: The effectiveness and sustainability of human-AI cooperation frequently hinge on the way people can gauge and
    trust AI agents. Sometimes, grave safety concerns arise from placing too much faith in automated systems. While much research
    has been done on the significance of system openness in maintaining appropriate adaptive trust calibration, more studies
    are still needed on detecting and mitigating erroneous trust measurement. Collaboration between humans and AI has become
    commonplace, bringing extremely complex AI systems into work environments. It is frequently ineffectual for human-AI because
    distorted perceptions, such as a lack of trust or comprehension, make it harder for users to follow the AI system’s suggestions.
    Our proposed approach to adaptive trust measurement entails a structure that monitors the user’s reliance behavior and
    cognitive behavior to identify the incorrect calibration state and advises the user to commence adaptive trust calibration
    again. The user’s mental capacity to perform complicated tasks determines how helpful an explanation will be. The experimental
    results demonstrate that users gain from having more power over their decisions and that explanations, if not adequately
    tailored to the individual user, may even be seen negatively by the user. An adaptive trust measurement has the potential
    to significantly aid in measuring trust for the governance of human-AI collaboration, thereby enhancing the effectiveness
    and sustainability of such collaborations.
