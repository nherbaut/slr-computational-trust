  title: Theoretical Framework of Engineering Decision-Making in Healthcare under the Influence of Social Media in the UAE
  authors: Hagar M. Mohamed, Mohamed Rashad, Mahmoud Khalifa
  abstract: Healthcare systems in the United Arab Emirates (UAE) are undergoing rapid transformation driven by engineering
    innovations such as artificial intelligence–powered diagnostics, telemedicine platforms, and robotic surgery. At the same
    time, social media platforms have become critical arenas for shaping public perceptions of these technologies, amplifying
    both trust and skepticism. This study develops a theoretical model that explains how social media discourse interacts
    with engineering decision-making in healthcare and influences technology adoption within the UAE context. Drawing on the
    Diffusion of Innovations Theory, the Social Amplification of Risk Framework (SARF), Institutional Theory, and trust models,
    the framework highlights the role of public perception as a mediator between online discourse and engineering choices,
    while also emphasizing the moderating effect of UAE policy and institutional context. The findings suggest that successful
    adoption of healthcare technologies requires not only technical excellence but also transparent communication, institutional
    legitimacy, and alignment with national strategies. By integrating engineering, media, and governance perspectives, this
    study contributes to advancing theoretical understanding of sociotechnical innovation and offers insights for policymakers,
    healthcare leaders, and engineers navigating the challenges of digital transformation in the UAE.
