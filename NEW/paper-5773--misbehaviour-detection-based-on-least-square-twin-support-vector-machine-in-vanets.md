  title: Misbehaviour Detection based on Least Square Twin Support Vector Machine in VANETs
  authors: Summia Parveen, S. V. Evangelin Sonia
  abstract: A great deal of study has not been done on machine learning techniques to identify inappropriate activities in
    Vehicular Adhoc Networks (VANETs). Unauthorized vehicles have the likely to send erroneous messages across VANET, which
    can lead to a difficult situation that cannot be avoided. This article describes a machine learning technique to classify
    misbehaviours in VANET. The proposed strategy is based on the Least Square Twin Support Vector Machine (LSTSVM), which
    the authors developed. Two of the mechanisms developed are called data based trust models (DTM) and vehicle based trust
    models (VTM). The data based trust model is responsible for identifying fraudulent messages by employing a LSTSVM-based
    classifier that evaluates message content in conjunction with vehicle characteristics. The message propagation behaviour
    of the vehicle is evaluated by the LSTSVM-based classifier utilized by the local VTM, which evaluation determines whether
    or not the vehicle may be trusted. Then, using the Dempster–Shafer theory, the trust authority (TA) VTM will compile several
    different trust assessment reports that pertain to the same vehicle and provide a comprehensive trust value. Compared
    to base classifiers utilized for classification, the LSTSVM approach is more effective in classifying a wide variety of
    inappropriate behaviours that can be discovered in VANET.
