  title: Trust Evaluation Model Based on PSO and LSTM for Huge Information Environments
  authors: Lin Zhang, Yanwen Huang, Xuan Jie, Xiong Fu, Lin Qiao-min, Wang Ruchuan
  abstract: Due to the challenge of increasing data volume, the traditional trust model is unable to manage data with high
    efficiency and effectively extract useful information hidden in big data. To fully utilize big data and combine machine
    learning with trust evaluation, a trust evaluation model based on Long short-term memory (LSTM) is presented. The powerful
    learning ability, expressive ability and dynamic timing of LSTM can be applied to study data while avoiding the vanishing
    and exploding gradient phenomena of traditional Recurrent neural networks (RNNs) to ensure that the model can learn sequences
    of random length and provide accurate trust evaluation. Targeting the performance instability caused by the LSTM model's
    random initialization of weights and thresholds, Particle swarm optimization (PSO), one of the intelligent algorithms,
    is introduced to find global optimal initial weights and thresholds. Experiments proved that the trust model proposed
    in this paper has high accuracy and contributes a new idea for trust evaluation in big data environments.
