  title: '[PDF] A Lightweight Smart Contract Framework for Behavior-Based Dynamic Identity Revocation in IoT Systems'
  authors: Ans Ibrahim Mahameed, Hussein Ahmed Ali, Karrar Oun Mohan, Fatima Sameer Hassan, Abbas Jalal Kaishesh, Zainab Marid
    Alzamili, ‪Mahmood A. Al-Shareeda‬‏, Mohammed Amin Almaiah, Rami Shehab
  abstract: 'With the Internet of Things (IoT) venturing into life and death area, distributing identity management has never
    become an important condition to meet for trust model such like security, auditability and being capable of failure. Traditional
    blockchain identity systems are mostly limited to registration, verification, without dynamic revocation and recovery
    features. In this paper, we propose a lightweight smart contract-driven framework to deal with dynamic trust based on
    real-time events in IoT networks. The research contribution is a modular architecture with which to programmatic revocation
    using behavior-based triggers, threshold consensus voting, and timelocked expiry as part of on-chain policy logic. In
    addition, a re-enrollment protocol tied to the certification ensures authenticity and lifetime after revocation. From
    a methodological point of view, the system is deployed as an Ethereum-based system and consists of 3 main building block
    components: (1) an Identity Registry, (2) a Revocation Policy Manager, and (3) an Access Monitor coupled with offchain
    oracles. The system guarantees constant-time (O(1)) lookups for identity checks and policy evaluation, that are protected
    by unforgeable event logs. Performance analysis shows that our proposed solution incurs low computational overhead (≤
    45 ms on ARM Cortex-M4), small communication cost (2.1 KB per transaction), and little gas consumption (≤ 115,000 units).
    The model achieves 2× speedup over state-of-the-art models EBIAS, Zhang et al., and Song et al. when preserving auditability
    and privacy. To summarize, the framework provides a scalable, secure and autonomous trust lifecycle management solution
    for IoT. The model will be extended in future with post-quantum cryptographic building blocks and machine learning—based
    anomaly scoring to develop the adaptive trust dynamics in real-world test beds.'
