  title: Research on Cooperative Spectrum Sensing based on Trust Scheme in Cognitive Radio Networks
  authors: Linna Hu, Rui Shi, Minghe Mao, Qingzi Wu, Zhiyu Chen, Jingchu Wang
  abstract: In cognitive wireless network, some selfish or malicious users will report incorrect spectrum sensing results
    during the spectrum sensing process, which will cause the fusion center to make wrong judgments. To solve this problem,
    we introduced a dynamic trust mechanism to reduce the influence of malicious users. A trust model based on Beta distribution
    is established to calculate the user trust level through the observational statistics in the perception phase. The Fusion
    Center assigns different weighting coefficients according to the trust of each user, and conducts a comprehensive evaluation
    of the historical trust status of cooperative users, thereby reducing the impact of sudden errors and improving the accuracy
    of cooperative spectrum sensing.
