  title: 'Probabilistic Inference at Scale: A Unified Approach for Cloud-Based Data Analytics'
  authors: Usman Ahmad Usmani, Junzo Watada, Fiza Beg
  abstract: We propose a mathematically rigorous solution to the problem of scalable probabilistic inference in cloud-based
    data analytics. Leveraging variational calculus and measuretheoretic probability, we derive provable bounds for approximate
    Bayesian inference under conditions of data partitioning, communication delays, and stochastic updates inherent in distributed
    cloud environments. Our analysis establishes sufficient conditions for convergence of variational posteriors when inference
    is performed asynchronously across geographically dispersed nodes. Central to our approach is a novel decomposition of
    the global evidence lower bound (ELBO) into tractable subproblems, each solvable within bounded communication budgets.
    We provide formal proofs demonstrating that, under mild assumptions, the distributed inference procedure asymptotically
    converges to the centralized Bayesian posterior. Moreover, we derive error bounds in total variation distance and KL divergence,
    quantifying the trade-off between inference accuracy and system-level constraints such as bandwidth, latency, and node
    reliability. Experimental results on large-scale datasets validate our theoretical claims, showcasing consistent improvements
    in convergence speed and inference fidelity. This work offers the first end-to-end proofbased treatment of scalable probabilistic
    inference tailored for modern cloud infrastructures.
