  title: 'Trust Factors in Recommendation Systems: Systematic Review Using SPAR-4-SLR with Bibliometric and ADO Analysis'
  authors: Shaista Alvi, Ruhi Sethi
  abstract: 'Recommender systems are essential in e-commerce for providing personalized suggestions, yet user trust remains
    a critical challenge affecting their effectiveness. This study conducts a systematic literature review using the SPAR-4-SLR
    protocol, combined with bibliometric analysis and the Antecedents-Decisions-Outcomes (ADO) framework, to analyze the evolution
    of trust in e-commerce recommendation systems from 2006 to 2025. The analysis of 84 publications identifies four primary
    research themes: trust-aware algorithms, e-commerce security, social trust propagation, and user behavior analysis. Findings
    reveal significant improvements in recommendation accuracy and user satisfaction, particularly in addressing cold-start
    problems. The study also uncovers geographic disparities in research impact, with Western nations leading in citations
    and emerging economies like China leading in publication volume. While challenges in scalability, privacy, and trust modeling
    persist, this review contributes a synthesized framework for understanding trust dynamics and proposes future research
    directions, including hybrid deep learning for trust propagation, blockchain for trust assessment, and the development
    of implicit, behavior-based trust models. This research provides a foundational roadmap for developing more reliable and
    effective trust-aware recommender systems.'
