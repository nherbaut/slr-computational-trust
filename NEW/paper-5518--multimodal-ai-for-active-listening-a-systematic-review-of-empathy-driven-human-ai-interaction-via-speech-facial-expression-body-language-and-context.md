  title: 'Multimodal AI for Active Listening: A Systematic Review of Empathy-Driven Human – AI Interaction via Speech, Facial
    Expression, Body Language, and Context'
  authors: Kasidit Asavakittikawin, Ekkarat Boonchieng
  abstract: Multimodal artificial intelligence (AI) systems have emerged as a powerful framework for building emotionally
    intelligent and empathetic human-AI interactions. This systematic review investigates the integration of multimodal cues—namely
    speech, facial expressions, body language, and contextual awareness—in designing AI systems capable of active listening
    and empathic behavior. Utilizing a PRISMA-inspired methodology, we analyze 30 peer-reviewed papers published between 2015
    and 2025, sourced from major academic databases. The results reveal that multimodal fusion, particularly when supported
    by large language models (LLMs), deep neural networks, and transformer architectures, significantly enhances an AI agent's
    ability to model emotional nuance and engage in affect-sensitive communication. Applications span mental health chatbots,
    educational tutors, social robots, and customer service agents. This review also highlights emerging trends in real-time
    emotion recognition, personalized affective feedback, and context-aware dialog management. The findings underscore the
    growing interdisciplinary convergence between affective computing, human-computer interaction, and AI ethics. Future directions
    include improving data diversity, modeling cultural variance in expression, and addressing ethical issues related to emotional
    manipulation and trust calibration in AI.
