  title: A Comparative Analysis of Trust Management Approaches for Attack Mitigation in IoT
  authors: Bhaskar Maithani, Ajay Vikram Singh, Said Broumi
  abstract: The Internet of Things (IoT) and Wireless Sensor Networks (WSN) underpin critical applications in healthcare,
    smart cities, and industrial automation, yet their resource-constrained nature exposes them to sophisticated attacks that
    evade conventional security measures. Trust management that assesses the behavior of nodes from either direct or indirect
    observations becomes a critical countermeasure. This survey reviews the various trust management methodologies — covering
    group-based, fuzzy logic, machine learning, blockchain, statistical, game-theoretic, time-series, hybrid, and social-based
    — and each is exemplified by a mainstay model. From reported results in the literature, fuzzy and hybrid trust models
    typically achieve detection accuracies above 85-92% against blackhole and selectiveforwarding attacks, while machine-learning-based
    trust models reach up to $\mathbf{9 0} \boldsymbol{-} \mathbf{9 6 \%}$ accuracy against Sybil and on-off attacks. We compare
    the strengths and drawbacks of such mechanisms through their performance in defending against a wide range of trust-based
    attacks on a gradient. Blockchain-enabled trust models provide strong tamper resistance but incur $\mathbf{2 0}-\mathbf{4
    0} \boldsymbol{\%}$ higher energy overhead compared to lightweight statistical and group-based schemes, which consume
    up to $25-50 \%$ less energy. It provides attack types, model mappings, and mitigation capabilities, serving as a guideline
    for academics and industrial researchers to develop trustworthy IoT/WSN-based systems.
