  title: A Comprehensive Survey on Blockchain, Federated Learning, and AI for Securing the Internet of Medical Things
  authors: Redjimi Mounira, Maatallah Majda, Benmachiche Abdelmadjid, Boufaida Soundes Oumaima, Makhlouf Derdour
  abstract: The Internet of Medical Things (IoMT) is transforming healthcare by enabling real-time monitoring and decentralized
    decision-making through interconnected medical devices, but collecting sensitive patient data creates a considerable privacy-related,
    security, and trust challenge. This survey provides a comprehensive overview of more recent initiatives that rely on Blockchain
    (BC), Federated Learning (FL), and Artificial Intelligence (AI) to assure IoMT systems. We classify the existing work
    into major technological families and examine systems that preserve patient privacy, decentralized authentication systems,
    and intrusion detection systems relying on AI models. Each approach has been analyzed based on its architectural design,
    use case, and security guarantees. Although recent progress has been made, we identify continued challenges in practical
    deployment, including computational complexity, interoperability issues, and regulatory constraints. The survey ends with
    a discussion outlining possible future developments, including lightweight consensus protocols for the Blockchain, post-quantum
    FL models, and scalable trust models for healthcare IoMT systems.
