  abstract: In order to augment traffic flow and safety, Vehicular Ad-hoc Networks (VANETs) allow infrastructure and automobiles
    to interact in real-time.Their reliance on wireless technology makes them susceptible to jamming attempts, which can severely
    limit or halt communication completely.Protecting infrastructure and vehicles from cyber threats requires ability to identify
    and stop assaults in VANET.It comprises utilising cryptographic technology, trust models, or machine learning to detect
    malicious behaviours such message tampering, spoofing, or Sybil assaults.Improved data integrity and smart transport system
    security are two outcomes of early detection.This study aimed to develop a novel Hybrid Deep Learning (HDL) model called
    Dense ABiLSTM (Attention-Based Bidirectional Long Short-Term Memory), use DSRC Vehicle Communication Dataset to detect
    jamming assaults in VANETs.Data preprocessing techniques including splitting datasets, normalising, and resampling are
    employed by pipeline to improve model's performance.Utilising Divine Religions Algorithm (DRA) for feature selection optimisation,
    to assess various classification representations, ranging from deep learning models like LSTM and 1D-CNN to more conventional
    algorithms like as SVM, KNN, DNN, and XG Boost.proposed model achieves better consequences than existing techniques in
    terms of F1-score, recall, accuracy, and precision.Furthermore, benchmarking is used to assess how feature selection strategies
    impact detection system's presentation.Binary Whale Optimisation (BWO), Grey Wolf Optimiser (GWO), and Genetic Algorithm
    (GA) are some of these approaches.area under curve (AUC-ROC), accuracy, recall, precision, and F1-score are some of evaluation
    metrics used to assess a model's presentation.experimental results show that Dense Net-ABiLSTM model outperforms both
    traditional and deep learning models in binary and multiclass classification tasks, making it a viable tool for detecting
    jamming attacks in VANETs.Binary classification achieved 98.7% accuracy, 0.986 F1-score, 0.987 recall, and 0.995 AUC,
    outperforming baseline models such as LSTM (94.7% accuracy) and XGBoost (95.3% accuracy).Multiclass classification (No-Jamming,
    Mild-Jamming, Severe-Jamming) achieved 96.8% accuracy, 0.967 macro F1-score, and 0.981 AUC, demonstrating robust performance
    across all classes.
