  title: Artificial Trust Model for Human-AI Teaming Systems
  authors: Clara Maathuis
  abstract: In this research, Artificial Trust (AT) is introduced by means of a novel computational ontology and an agent-based
    simulation framework in the context of Human-AI Teaming (HAT) systems. This implies pivoting from the traditional human
    trust in AI (Artificial Intelligence) paradigm to a machine-trust-in-human and machine-trust-in-system perspective, where
    trust is treated as a computing state regulating autonomy under uncertainty. To this end, AT is formalized as the AI agent's
    quantified certainty regarding the human agent's capability and integrity and the collective team's reliability to achieve
    a shared objective. Furthermore, in order to capture the emergent, nonlinear dynamics of trust in operational contexts,
    the complexity science approach is adopted within an agent-based simulation that models trust as a stochastic, time evolving
    variable shaped by heterogeneous agents, feedback loops, and contextual stressors where autonomy behaviors emerge from
    transitions between supportive and protective stances as trust fluctuates. Hence, this model provides a foundation for
    analyzing trust calibration, intervention policies, and resilient teaming designs in Human-AI systems while ensuring that
    responsible behavior is promoted in this context at both individual and collective agent levels.
