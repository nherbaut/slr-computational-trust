  title: AI-Powered Zero Trust Architecture and Anomaly Detection to Protect 5G Core Networks
  authors: Sharma Ji, Vishal Jayaswal, Sandeep Yadav, Harnit Saini, Sharvan Kumar, Eshank Jain
  abstract: Severe cybersecurity risks in the guise of Distributed Denial-of-Service (DDoS) attacks, insider attacks, and
    advanced persistent threats have been forged due to the widespread expansion of 5G networks. Since 5G core networks are
    dynamic, decentralized, and software-defined, they cannot be adequately protected by traditional perimeter-based security
    controls. To strengthen network security, Zero Trust Architecture (ZTA) uses least-privilege access control, micro-segmentation,
    and strict identity verification. Static ZTA rules, however, are powerless to recognize and nullify changing threats in
    real time. To offset this, we introduce an AI-based security architecture that marries the principle of Zero Trust and
    deep learning-based anomaly detection, i.e., through the utilization of Temporal Convolutional Networks (TCN). To further
    build resilience, our model dynamically fine-tunes security settings, continuously monitors network activity, and identifies
    malicious activity. In comparison to traditional machine learning-based detection, experimental tests on actual 5G traffic
    datasets reveal that it provides 45% fewer false positives and 99.1% detection rate and 98.7% F1-score. In 5G core networks,
    the solution enhances adaptive defense features, reduces the impact of attacks, and enhances real-time threat detection.
    These findings illustrate how AI-driven Zero Trust models can arm next-generation communications networks with highly
    advanced, real-time security enforcement.
