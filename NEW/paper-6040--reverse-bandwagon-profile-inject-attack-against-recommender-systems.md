  title: Reverse Bandwagon Profile Inject Attack against Recommender Systems
  authors: Fuguo Zhang
  abstract: Collaborative filtering algorithms are successfully used in personalized recommender systems for their simplicity
    and high recommending quality. However, significant vulnerabilities have recently been identified in collaborative filtering
    recommender systems. Malicious users can inject a large number of biased profiles into such a system in order to make
    recommendations that favor or disfavor given items. The reverse bandwagon attack is considered to need low knowledge cost.
    In this paper, we examine the robustness of our topic-level trust-based recommendation algorithm that incorporate topic-level
    trust model into classic collaborative filtering algorithm under the reverse bandwagon attack. The results of our experiments
    show that topic-level trust based Collaborative Filtering algorithm offers significant improvements in stability over
    the standard k-nearest neighbor approach when attacked.
