  title: A Run-Length Coding Based Dynamic Trust Model in P2P Network
  authors: Qun Fang, Yi Ji, Guoxin Wu
  abstract: The critical challenges for peer-to-peer (P2P) systems must be the task to manage risks in interacting with unknown
    peers. Reputation-based trust management can decrease this risk by evaluating trustworthiness of a certain peer from its
    historic behaviors. However, many existing trust models do not provide adequate reaction to quick changes in peers¿ behavior,
    showing their ineffectiveness coping with dynamic malicious peers. In this paper, we propose a run-length-coding based
    trust model - RunTrust - computing trust degree based on behaviors history in P2P networks. In particular, our proposed
    model is capable to detect and penalize both the sudden changes in peers¿ behaviors and their potential oscillatory malicious
    behavior. Simulation shows our model¿s unique features and advantages over the existing models.
