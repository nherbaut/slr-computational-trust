  title: 'Leveraging AI to Forge Zero Trust Security in IoT Systems: Smart Homes Case'
  authors: Danya AbuALRish, Radwan Tahboub
  abstract: Smart meters are advanced Internet of Things (IoT) devices used to measure energy consumption in real time, improving
    the efficiency of energy systems. However, as these devices become interconnected, they are increasingly exposed to cyber-security
    threats and challenges. Traditional security methods are insufficient due to the dynamic nature of their interconnectivity,
    where devices continuously interact and exchange data. The Zero Trust Model (ZTM) has emerged as a promising security
    solution, operating on the principle of "Never Trust, Always Verify." This study proposes a ZTM-based approach to securing
    IoT systems. By leveraging Artificial Intelligence (AI) techniques—including Decision Trees, K-mean, and Isolation Forest—the
    proposed model continuously authenticates and isolates devices based on user behavior and consumption patterns. Additionally,
    it develops behavior profiles to monitor and analyze consumption trends, enabling real-time anomaly detection and mitigation
    of security threats. The integrated ZTM with Decision Tree outperformed other anomaly detection models with the highest
    98.4% accuracy, recall, precision, and F1-score. In contrast, ZTM integrated with Isolation Forest (67% accuracy, F1-score
    67%), while ZTM combined with K-Mean performed worst, with (47% accuracy, F1-score 35%) less effective. The ZTM with the
    Decision Tree model had the lowest false positive and false negative rates nearly zero. The standalone ZTM model (without
    AI integration) exhibited the longest training and testing times, as well as the highest memory usage—2343.61 MB during
    training and 585.92 MB during testing, Despite its slower, it was still effective in identifying power consumption anomalies,
    including device malfunctions and potential energy theft.In comparison, the ZTM models integrated with the Decision Tree
    and K-mean were faster and more memory-efficient. Among them, ZTM with Decision Tree demonstrated the best trade-off between
    speed and accuracy. Although Isolation Forest is theoretically more advanced, it performed poorly in both detection accuracy
    and computational efficiency.While the standalone ZTM was slower, it was still effective in identifying power consumption
    anomalies, including device malfunctions and potential energy theft.In Addition, The proposed model was compared with
    two other models based on blockchain technology for protecting the data of smart meters. These findings underscore the
    significance of applying ZTM with AI to enhance IoT security in smart homes. Beyond improving security, this approach
    also promotes more efficient energy management, contributing to the development of sustainable smart cities.
