  title: Framework to Integrating IDS, Machine Learning, ZTM, and Two-Factor Authentication into IoMT Systems
  authors: Basma Mohammad Abu Sabha, Radwan Tahboub
  abstract: 'The growing use of the Internet of Medical Things (IoMT) in ambulances enables real-time transmission of patients’
    vital signs to hospitals. However, this exchange is increasingly exposed to cyber threats, especially Distributed Denial
    of Service (DDoS) and data injection or spoofed attacks that can delay critical decisions and compromise patient safety.
    While many existing solutions focus on securing medical data through encryption or blockchain in a mobile medical environment,
    they do not focus on detecting anomalies during transmission or analyzing network and data content behavior in real-time.This
    paper proposes a lightweight, real-time security framework for Emergency Medical Services (EMS) that monitors both network
    behavior and data content behavior to detect and isolate threats early before data is transmitted to the hospital. The
    model integrates Machine Learning (ML), a Signature Intrusion Detection System (SIDS), the Zero Trust Model (ZTM), and
    Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) to identify attacks at two levels: (1) DDoS attacks at the network level and (2) data
    injection attacks at the content level. Once an attack is detected, the system applies an isolation mechanism that either
    blocks the compromised device at the ambulance or disconnects the ambulance’s network interface, preventing further propagation
    of malicious traffic. ML classifiers such as Decision Tree (DT) and Random Forest (RF) are trained in the cloud (hospital)
    and deployed at the edge (ambulance) to enable fast response and resource efficient operation. The initial results at
    the data level show that the system achieved up to 99.03% accuracy in selecting the correct response action, accurately
    selecting the proper response, isolating the IoMT device, alerting security, or continuing data transmission.'
