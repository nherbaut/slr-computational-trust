  title: Trust-Aware Professional Identity Representation in AI-Mediated Systems
  authors: Shubham Anandani
  abstract: As artificial intelligence systems increasingly participate in evaluating, ranking, and recommending professionals,
    digital identity representations play a critical role in shaping opportunity access and trust formation. Traditional profiles
    emphasize static credentials, yet fail to capture trust-related signals that humans implicitly rely upon. This paper proposes
    a trust-aware conceptual framework for professional identity representation, grounded in established trust theory and
    adapted for AI-mediated environments. Building on organizational trust models, we introduce a four-dimensional construct-Authenticity,
    Credibility, Empathy, and Uniqueness (ACE-U)-and analyze its implications for the design of scalable, machine-interpretable
    identity systems. We discuss how each dimension can be operationalized through observable signals, examine interactions
    and trade-offs among the dimensions, and outline system-level considerations for responsible AI evaluation.
