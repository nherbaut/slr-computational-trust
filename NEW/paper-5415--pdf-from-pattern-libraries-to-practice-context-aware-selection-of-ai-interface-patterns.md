  title: '[PDF] From Pattern Libraries to Practice: Context-Aware Selection of AI Interface Patterns'
  authors: Thomas Van den Bossche, Abasin Abdul Khalil
  abstract: 'AI interface pattern libraries provide valuable catalogs, but practitioners lack decision support for selecting
    patterns that fit specific contexts. We introduce a context-aware selection framework that translates situational factors
    (user, task, interface, regulatory context) into priorities over four design dimensions: trust calibration, user control,
    transparency, and personalization, linking these to concrete pattern choices. We apply the framework to AI-assisted form
    authoring, where high subjectivity and data-protection constraints prioritize user control, motivating per-field, on-demand
    suggestions over system-initiated automation. A preliminary mixed-method pilot (N = 6) provides converging signals: four
    participants preferred on-demand suggestions and described an authoring stance (“choosing a direction”) rather than editing
    (“checking for errors”). These findings motivate a larger cross-domain study to test whether coarse context assessments
    predict calibrated reliance behavior.'
