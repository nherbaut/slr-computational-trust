  title: '[PDF] Building Trustworthy AI Systems for Condition Monitoring'
  authors: Aleksanteri Hämäläinen, Aku Karhinen, Raine Viitala
  abstract: Various institutions have issued guidelines on how AI models should be implemented and used to ensure safety,
    reliability, transparency, and legal compliance, fostering public trust. Despite this, guidelines concerning AI models
    in industrial processes such as machine condition monitoring remain sparse. This paper provides an overview of a machine
    learning-enhanced, human-in-the-loop condition monitoring process, with discussion on how each stage affects the reliability
    and trustworthiness of both the machine learning model itself and the human-AI dyad formed by the model and its user.
    We synthesize existing best practices for specifically condition monitoring and propose techniques that may support trust
    calibration through better explainability of a model’s predictions. This paper serves as a guideline to support the development
    of machine learning-enhanced condition monitoring systems that take into account the essential aspects of trust and have
    the potential to earn the justified trust of its users, both primary and secondary.
