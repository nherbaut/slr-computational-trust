  title: '[PDF] GDPR Constraints on AI-Generated Music: Privacy Protection Challenges in Training Data'
  authors: N. Liu
  abstract: With the rapid development of artificial intelligence music generation technology, its application in the creative
    industry has raised significant challenges to privacy compliance. This article focuses on the privacy compliance issues
    of AI music generation technology under the framework of the EU GDPR, revealing the uniqueness of this field that distinguishes
    it from traditional AI privacy research. Research has found that the emotional attributes and creative characteristics
    of music data subject it to more complex compliance requirements than conventional data, and existing anonymization technologies
    have significant deficiencies in protecting the privacy of music data. Through case comparison and analysis, this study
    proposes a novel combined scheme based on technologies such as federated learning and synthetic data generation to construct
    a multi-layer protection system. The solution can effectively address the special privacy risks of music data, and at
    the same time demonstrate the unique value of the data trust model in balancing artistic innovation and privacy protection.
    Compared with existing literature, this study systematically explores for the first time the privacy protection challenges
    in the specific AI application scenario of music generation, providing new ideas for compliance practices in the creative
    industry. Research indicates that the sustainable development of the AI music industry requires the establishment of a
    new governance framework that takes into account both technological innovation and legal regulation.
