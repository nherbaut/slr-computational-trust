  title: 'Influence of Artificial Intelligence on Managerial Decision Systems and Trust Formation: A Mixed-Methods Study'
  authors: Jofia Jose Prakash, Dilpal Singh Chhabra, Aakash, Rohitha Gajam, Abhishek Bhushan Singhal
  abstract: The fast embedding of Artificial Intelligence (AI) into the organizational decisionmaking process has changed
    the essence of managerial thinking, power, and responsibility. Although AI-based decision support systems are associated
    with increased efficiency, predictive accuracy, and real-time analytics, they also create serious issues in terms of transparency,
    explainability, and the establishment of trust between managerial stakeholders. This research experimentally examines
    how AI-based managerial decision systems affect intensification of trust, perceived trustworthiness and intentions to
    adopt the systems, through a mixed methods research design. Structured instruments to measure system explainability, decision
    confidence, and algorithmic trust were used to gather quantitative data regarding 327 mid-level and senior managers in
    technology, finance, healthcare, and manufacturing sectors. The relationships were tested using structural equation modelling
    (SEM). The qualitative data of 24 semi-structured interviews formed the basis of qualitative information to put into perspective
    the cognitive and emotional processes of trust calibration through AI mediated settings. It is shown that the perceived
    explainability, the perceived procedural transparency, and the human-in-the-loop mechanisms are strong predictors of trust
    in the AI systems, which, in turn, mediate their effects on the decision acceptance and continuity in its use. The paper
    is relevant to the growing debate on reliable AI by providing an integrative model that brings together technical qualities
    and psychological and organizational aspects of the trust. The practical implications of the design of ethical, interpretable
    and manager-centric AI systems are addressed.
