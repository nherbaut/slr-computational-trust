  title: 'Operationalizing Generative and Agentic AI Across Complex Logistics Networks: Architecture, Governance, and Trust
    Models'
  authors: Nirmal Kumar Jingar
  abstract: Contemporary logistics networks are extremely dynamic, uncertain and distributed settings in which traditional
    optimization systems are ineffective in responding to disruptions, unstable demand and coordination across stakeholders.
    Current logistics intelligence systems tend to operate in unconnected pipelines and do not have inbuilt forecasts capability,
    decentralized decision-making processes, and trust mechanisms driven by governance. In this paper introduce the Agentic
    Generative AI Framework (AGAF) which is an integrated architecture integrating generative artificial intelligence, autonomous
    decision agents and a policy-controlled trust layer to improve the operational resilience and efficiency of large logistics
    ecosystems. Experimental analyses demonstrate that AGAF has been found to be better than the current systems in terms
    of accuracy of forecasting, reduction of costs, response to disruption, efficiency in coordination, compliance, and mitigation
    of risks. The results indicate the possibility of combining generative intelligence with agentic autonomy in order to
    create trustful, adaptive and scalable logistics networks.
