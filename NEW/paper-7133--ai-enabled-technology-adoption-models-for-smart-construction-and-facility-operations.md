  title: AI-Enabled Technology Adoption Models for Smart Construction and Facility Operations
  authors: Saleem Ahmed Al-Azazi, Musmuliadi Kamaruding, Mohammed Abdulrazzaq Alaghbari, Amena Y. Mutahar, Khalil Idrissi
    Gartoumi, Abdullah O. Baarimah
  abstract: 'Artificial intelligence (AI) capabilities are advancing rapidly across the built-environment lifecycle, enabling
    computer vision-based progress and safety monitoring, predictive analytics, and digital twin-driven decision support.
    Despite these advances, AI adoption in construction and facility operations frequently stalls beyond pilot deployments
    due to fragmented stakeholder structures, high safety and liability exposure, unstable data pipelines, and persistent
    discontinuities between construction delivery and operational handover. Existing technology adoption models explain intention
    and use primarily through perceived usefulness and organizational readiness, but they remain insufficient for AI-enabled
    systems that introduce additional requirements related to trust calibration, explainability, cybersecurity and privacy,
    accountability, and continuous performance monitoring. This paper presents a framework-development and validationprotocol
    study for a governance-aware, lifecycle, multi-level adoption model for AI-enabled smart construction and facility operations.
    The model integrates classical adoption theory with trustworthy AI governance constructs and explicitly accounts for construction-to-operations
    handover continuity. Using a theory-driven integrative synthesis, the paper organizes determinants across five blocks-Technology
    & Data, Human & Trust, Organization & Governance, Ecosystem & Institutional, and Lifecycle/Handover-and specifies adoption
    outcomes across three stages: intention, implementation success, and continuance. Rather than claiming completed large-sample
    SEM results, the manuscript defines a multistakeholder survey design and a PLS-SEM/MGA reporting protocol, with optional
    Delphi-based refinement, to test lifecycle-contingent and role-specific effects in subsequent empirical work. The paper
    contributes a unified explanatory framework, a theory-grounded measurement instrument, and an actionable adoption readiness
    scorecard that supports safer, more sustainable, and scalable AI adoption across the builtenvironment lifecycle.'
