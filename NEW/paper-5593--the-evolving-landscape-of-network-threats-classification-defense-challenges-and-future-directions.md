  title: 'The Evolving Landscape of Network Threats: Classification, Defense Challenges, and Future Directions'
  authors: Nabanita Roy, Raj Gaurang Tiwari, Sangita Roy, Ambuj Kumar Agarwal, Amit Garg, Neema Gupta
  abstract: Cybersecurity threats have evolved at an unprecedented pace, posing significant challenges to individuals, organizations,
    and government institutions. This research addresses the critical issue of the growing sophistication and diversity of
    network attacks, which exploit vulnerabilities in both conventional and modern digital systems. Through a comprehensive
    investigation, It gives the the detailed classification of network attacks, analyzing their operational methods, impacts,
    and corresponding defense mechanisms. This research highlights the limitations of traditional security measures in combating
    advanced threats such as malware, phishing, ransomware, and Advanced Persistent Threats. It also identifies key defensive
    challenges, including the need for adaptive and scalable solutions, and explores the transformative potential of Machine
    Learning (ML) and Artificial Intelligence (AI) in enhancing threat detection and prevention. The outcomes of this work
    include a systematic taxonomy of network attacks, an evaluation of emerging defense technologies (e.g., blockchain, Zero
    Trust models), and actionable recommendations for future research to strengthen cybersecurity resilience.
