  title: Comprehensive Evaluations of Machine Learning enabled Trust and Reputation Models in Internet of Things
  authors: Manpreet Kaur, Vinod Kumar Verma
  abstract: The Internet of Things (IoT) stands out as a pervasive technology, exerting a profound influence on various facets
    of our lives including social, commercial, and economic dimensions. IoT technologies hold immense potential for elevating
    human life. Despite these transformative benefits, the prevalence of cyber-attacks and threats poses considerable challenges
    to the security of IoT applications. Conventional standalone security measure (cryptography) have proven inadequate in
    heterogeneous and resource constraint IoT networks. To effectively tackle these challenges, machine learning (ML) techniques
    are applied in trust management for IoT security. This paper provides a comprehensive analysis of support vector machine
    (SVM) based models employed for trust management, to address challenges associated with trust evaluation in IoT. Furthermore,
    we examine existing research to demonstrate the effectiveness and applicability of ML in IoT trust management. The paper
    concludes with future research directions and potential advancements in this field.
